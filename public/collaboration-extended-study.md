# The Art of Effective Collaboration: Extended Technical Study

## Lab Module MOD-002 | Stage Lab | Choreography II

---

## Overview

This module guides you into the practice of effective, intentional collaboration — with dancers, composers, and interdisciplinary partners who shape the choreographic world around you.

**The Goal:** Give you a roadmap for how to enter, navigate within, make decisions inside, and exit collaborative processes with clarity and artistic integrity.

---

# SECTION ONE: Entering the Collaborative Process

## 1. Planning a Collaboration Session

Before you walk into the room:

- **Define your goals** for the session
- **Identify immediate needs** of your piece
- **Prepare questions** you must ask
- **Consider roles and expectations** of all partners

## 2. Understanding Your Collaborators

### Dancers
- **Timeline:** Throughout the process — most consistent presence
- **They need:** Clear vocabulary, space for interpretation, timely feedback, safety
- **You need:** Embodied feedback, creative contribution, honest communication
- **Communication:** Direct but supportive. Use body-based language.

### Composers
- **Timeline:** Early concept phase + lab sessions + final refinement
- **They need:** Clear intention, flexibility with timing, reference materials, trust
- **You need:** Drafts and iterations, responsiveness to movement, flexibility
- **Communication:** Sensory language — texture, weight, atmosphere.

### Designers
- **Timeline:** Concept phase → Design phase → Tech week
- **They need:** Early concepts, visual references, budget clarity, decisiveness
- **You need:** Renderings, technical possibilities, problem-solving
- **Communication:** Visual — bring images, sketches, videos.

### Technical Partners
- **Timeline:** Pre-production + Tech week (intensive)
- **They need:** Advance notice, clear cue sheets, respect for load-in time
- **You need:** Technical solutions, safety guidance, timing precision
- **Communication:** Specific and practical. Ask questions early.

## 3. Building Your Research Bank

Create a collection system for:
- Videos
- Articles
- Images
- Sound files
- Choreographic scores

**Digital:** Organized drive with clear folder structure
**Physical:** Journal, diagrams, logs

## 4. The Energy Check-In

Before entering collaboration, assess yourself:

1. **How clear is my intention right now?** (1-5)
2. **How open am I to unexpected directions?** (1-5)
3. **How is my body feeling?** (1-5)
4. **What do I need to protect today?**

If your average is below 3, consider what you need before entering.

## 5. The Collaboration Contract

Make a contract with yourself:

- **My guiding question today:** What am I trying to discover?
- **What I will protect:** What is non-negotiable?
- **What I am open to:** Where can I be surprised?
- **My signal to pause:** How will I know when to step back?

# SECTION TWO: Navigating Within Collaboration

## 1. The Language of Impact

**Directive language** closes doors. **Impact language** opens them.

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Make it louder" | "I'm imagining more presence — what would that feel like?" |
| "That's not working" | "Something feels unresolved — can we explore alternatives?" |
| "Do it faster" | "What happens if we compress the time between moments?" |
| "I don't like that" | "I'm not connecting yet — help me understand your intention?" |
| "That's wrong" | "That's different from what I imagined — let's clarify together" |

**The Pattern:**
1. Share your observation without judgment
2. Express curiosity about their perspective
3. Invite shared exploration

## 2. Tone Matters

The same note can close doors or open them:

**Closed:** "That transition needs to be sharper."
*Effect: Directive, leaves no room for input*

**Neutral:** "Let's look at the transition."
*Effect: Opens space but doesn't invite collaboration*

**Open:** "I'm curious about the transition — what are you feeling there?"
*Effect: Invites dialogue, shares ownership*

## 3. Transform vs. Change

**Transform (Shape It):**
- Keep the core, adjust the execution
- Ask: "What if we tried it with...?"
- Build on what exists
- Maintain momentum

**Change (Abandon It):**
- Start fresh with new direction
- Say: "Let's try something different"
- Risk losing good material
- Can disrupt trust

**Default to transformation.** Change only when fundamentally misaligned.

## 4. When to Initiate, Clarify, or Transform

- **Initiate** new concepts when there's space and energy
- **Clarify** when you sense misalignment — don't let confusion build
- **Transform** when an idea has potential but needs shaping
- **Protect** your artistic intention while remaining genuinely open

# SECTION THREE: The Collaborative Lab

## Working With Composers in Real Time

### Work With Intention

Enter with a guiding question broad enough to spark multiple directions:

*"How might the sound create a sense of suspension — like the moment before a decision?"*

Your question should allow flexibility, discovery, and layered interpretation.

### What to Bring

- Journal or notebook
- Pencils or markers
- Phone recorder (audio + video)
- Audiovisual inspirations
- Prepared questions
- Space for noting unexpected suggestions

### Questions to Hold

1. What is my piece asking for?
2. What is unclear?
3. What could grow here?

### Using Immediate Resources

- **Classroom technology:** Speakers, projectors, cameras
- **Professors and TAs:** Conceptual sounding boards
- **Classmates:** Thought partners
- **The studio:** Testing ground for movement
- **Props/objects:** Generative tools
- **Improvisation:** Research in action

# SECTION FOUR: Exiting Through Reflection

## 1. Identifying Takeaways

After each session, ask:

- What ideas **strengthened** the piece?
- What **surprised** you?
- What felt **misaligned**, and why?
- What elements created **tension or friction**?

## 2. Tension Mapping

A tension is not a problem — it's information.

Map tensions on two axes:
- **X-axis:** Artistic ←→ Interpersonal
- **Y-axis:** Needs attention now ←→ Can hold for later

This helps you see: where is energy living in my collaboration?

## 3. Distilling and Navigating Tensions

- Separate emotional discomfort from artistic misalignment
- Hold unresolved ideas without forcing closure
- Communicate through tension rather than around it

## 4. When Things Don't Go Your Way

- Collaboration is co-presence, not control
- Know when to advocate for your intention
- Know when to listen and adjust
- Protect your mental energy during difficult sessions

## 5. Caring for Yourself

Because collaboration can feel extractive, disorienting, or compromising:

- **Daily check-ins:** "What worked? What changed? Is this aligned?"
- **Emotional regulation:** Give yourself time to process
- **Permission to pause:** You don't have to solve everything today
- **Body-care:** Movement, rest, nourishment

## 6. Healthy Closure

- Log what's unresolved (without solving it)
- Identify points of return
- Exit without emotional residue
- Prepare mentally to re-enter the next process

- How to **intentionally plan and enter** a collaboration
- How to **work inside** a collaboration with clarity, openness, and integrity
