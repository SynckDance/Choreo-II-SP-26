Goal

A
Camera Fundamentals
🎥 Technical Settings
  • Resolution: shoot consistent quality, do not mix formats
  • Frame rate: decide based on movement needs
  • Shutter: avoid flicker and blown highlights
  • White balance: lock it for consistency
  • Stabilization: tripod/gimbal when needed, handheld when meaningful
🖼️ Composition for Dance
  • Wide shots show phrase architecture
  • Close shots show breath, sweat, tension, detail
  • Camera height changes power dynamics
  • Background matters: clean reads as intentional
B
Sound + Lighting
🎤 Sound Capture
Even if you plan to replace sound in post, capture clean location sound.
  • Record room tone
  • Capture footsteps and breath if they matter
  • Avoid wind and noisy HVAC
💡 Lighting on Location
  • Natural light changes fast, plan around it
  • Backlight can silhouette too hard
  • Avoid mixed lighting unless intentional
  • Goal: dancers must be readable
C
Performance + Care
❤️ Dancer Safety Critical
  • Warm-up and cooldown
  • Break schedule
  • Floor safety
  • Temperature and hydration
  • Respect dancers as collaborators, not objects

Goal

Post-production is choreography. Editing is rhythm. Sound is world-building.

✂️ Edit Workflow
  • Organize footage first
  • Create rough cut (structure first)
  • Build rhythm and pacing
  • Cut on breath, impact, stillness, transitions
  • Picture lock: stop changing structure once locked
🔊 Sound Design
Choose a direction:
  • Music-driven cut
  • Location sound-driven (documentary)
  • Sound design-driven (foley, textures)

Sound must support the dance. Avoid using sound as distraction.

🎨 Color
  • Correct: exposure and white balance
  • Grade: mood and style
  • Keep skin tones believable unless intentionally stylized
📜 Titles + Credits
  • Film title
  • Cast list
  • Creative roles
  • Music and licensing credits
  • Location credits if needed

Technical Craft Toolkit

This page is not meant to overwhelm you. It is meant to give you a clear menu of technical decisions.

📷 Camera + Capture
  • Camera types: phone, DSLR, mirrorless, cinema
  • Lenses: wide, normal, telephoto
  • Stabilization: tripod, gimbal, handheld, dolly
  • Frame rates: normal vs slow motion
  • Focus: auto vs manual (based on scene)
🏠 Location + Production Design
Location is choreography. Walls, floors, texture, and distance all shape movement.
  • Props and objects
  • Environment and space
  • Wardrobe and color palette
  • If your film has a "world," design that world with care
💡 Lighting Basics
  • Natural: golden hour, shade, direct sun
  • Artificial: practical lamps, LED panels, studio lights
  • Avoid flat overhead unless intentionally raw
  • Understand: key light, fill light, back light
  • Goal: dancers must be readable
🎤 Sound Capture
Audio matters more than people think. If audio is unusable, the film reads amateur fast.
  • Capture clean audio
  • Or plan sound design intentionally
  • Room tone, footsteps, breath
✂️ Editing + Color
  • Editing is choreographic structure
  • Color correction is clarity
  • Color grading is mood
🎬 Shot Types Gallery
  • Wide: shows phrase architecture, full body, space
  • Medium: torso up, relationship, gesture
  • Close: face, hands, feet, detail
  • Detail: texture, sweat, fabric, floor
  • Cutaway: environment, objects, transitions
💡

Beginner vs Advanced

Start simple. One camera, natural light, clean audio. Add complexity only when you can control it. Advanced techniques without control create chaos, not art.

Casting + Performance

Stage presence and camera presence are not the same thing. Cast for the work, not just for friendship.

A
Casting Choices
❓ Core Questions
  • Who carries the film?
  • Do you need virtuosity, softness, rawness, acting, stillness?
  • Technical ability, emotional ability, or both?
  • Do not cast only your friends. Cast for the work.

C
Rehearsal for Film
🎥 Camera Rehearsal
  • Rehearse with camera early
  • Rehearse transitions and re-takes
  • Build endurance for multiple takes
  • Practice "small performance" for close-ups
  • Practice stillness. Stillness reads loud on camera.
📊 Camera Test Rubric
Rate 1-5 on:
  • Camera presence (reads clearly on screen)
  • Movement clarity in frame
  • Adaptability to direction
  • Relationship to camera
  • Professionalism and care
